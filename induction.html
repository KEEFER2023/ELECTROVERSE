🧲 Magnetism & Electromagnetic Induction

Explore the full story of magnetism and induction — from magnetic fields and forces to energy generation and high-efficiency electrical systems.

1.1. Introduction to Magnetism

Magnetism arises from moving charges and intrinsic magnetic moments of particles. It's foundational in electric motors, transformers, inductors, and storage devices.

The magnetic field B is measured in tesla (T).

1.2. Magnetic Fields from Currents

  • Biot–Savart Law: dB = (μ₀ / 4π) × (I dl × r̂) / r²
  • Ampère’s Law: ∮ B · dl = μ₀ I_enc
Example: Straight wire, current I → B = (μ₀ I) / (2πr)

1.3. Fields from Loops, Solenoids, Toroids

  • Loop: B = (μ₀ I) / (2R) at center
  • Solenoid: B = μ₀ n I (n = turns/length)
  • Toroid: B = (μ₀ N I) / (2πr) inside core

1.4. Lorentz Force on Charges

F = q (v × B) — Right-hand rule for direction

Example: Electron in B-field → helical motion

1.5. Force on Current-Carrying Wire

F = I (L × B)

Loop in uniform B-field → torque → τ = μ × B

1.6. Magnetic Materials

  • Ferromagnetic: Strong alignment (Fe, Co, Ni)
  • Paramagnetic: Weak, temporary alignment
  • Diamagnetic: Weak opposition to field

1.7. Real Engineering Uses

  • Motors: Field–current interaction
  • Transformers: Mutual induction
  • Inductors: Magnetic energy storage
  • Magnetic sensors, shields

1.8. Introduction to Electromagnetic Induction

Changing magnetic fields induce voltage (emf) in loops and conductors — the principle behind all electrical generators.

1.9. Faraday’s and Lenz’s Laws

𝓔 = - dΦ/dt

Where Φ = ∫ B · dA is magnetic flux. Minus sign → Lenz’s law: opposes the cause.

2.0. EMF from Motion

𝓔 = B L v
Rod of 1 m in 0.3 T, moving at 5 m/s → 𝓔 = 0.3 × 1 × 5 = 1.5 V

2.1. Rotating Loops and AC Generation

𝓔 = N B A ω sin(ωt)

This gives sinusoidal voltage output in generators.

2.2. Eddy Currents

Circulating currents in bulk metal oppose motion → cause magnetic damping or heating.

Used in brakes, induction cookers, metal detectors

2.3. Inductance

  • Self-induction: 𝓔 = -L (dI/dt)
  • Mutual induction: 𝓔₂ = -M (dI₁/dt)

2.4. Engineering Applications

  • Transformers
  • Wireless charging
  • Power generation
  • Induction heating
