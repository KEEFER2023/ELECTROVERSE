🔧 Operational Amplifiers (Op-Amps)

Operational Amplifiers are versatile and fundamental components used in analog electronics for amplification, signal conditioning, filtering, and mathematical operations.

🧩 Op-Amp Basics

  • High gain differential amplifier
  • High input impedance, low output impedance
  • Two inputs: Inverting (−) and Non-inverting (+)
  • Output = A × (V+ − V−) where A is open-loop gain

🌀 Op-Amp Golden Rules (Ideal)

  • Input current is zero: I+ = I− = 0
  • Input voltages are equal: V+ = V−

⚙️ Common Configurations

  • Inverting Amplifier: Gain = −Rf/Rin
  • Non-inverting Amplifier: Gain = 1 + Rf/R1
  • Unity Gain Buffer: Output = Input (used for impedance matching)
  • Summing Amplifier: Output = −(V1/R1 + V2/R2) × Rf
  • Differential Amplifier: Amplifies voltage difference between inputs

💡 Applications

  • Signal conditioning
  • Analog filters (low-pass, high-pass)
  • Voltage followers
  • Integrators and differentiators
  • Analog math operations: add, subtract, multiply
  • Oscillators and waveform generators

📝 Practice Quiz

What is the output voltage of an ideal op-amp in a unity gain buffer?

Answer: Vout = Vin

What is the gain of an inverting amplifier with Rf = 100kΩ and Rin = 20kΩ?

Answer: −5

What is the purpose of a differential amplifier?

Answer: To amplify the difference between two input signals
