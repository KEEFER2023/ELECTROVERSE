💾 Digital Electronics

Digital electronics deals with circuits that operate using discrete signals — typically binary — to represent logical and numerical values. It’s the foundation of computers, microcontrollers, digital communication, and control systems.

🧮 Binary Number System

The binary number system (base-2) uses only two digits: 0 and 1. Each digit is referred to as a bit. All digital circuits ultimately store and process data using binary.

Binary is the language of computers and digital logic systems. All arithmetic and logic operations boil down to manipulating bits.

📗 Decimal ↔ Binary Conversion

  • To convert decimal to binary: divide by 2 repeatedly and track remainders.
  • To convert binary to decimal: multiply each bit by 2position and sum.
Example: Convert 1310 to binary:
13 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 1
6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0
3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1
1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1

🧮 Binary Arithmetic

  • Addition: 0+0=0, 0+1=1, 1+1=10 (carry 1), 1+1+1=11
  • Subtraction: Use borrow method (or complement method)
  • Multiplication: Like decimal long multiplication but only 0s and 1s
  • Division: Long division method in binary

🔄 Signed Binary Numbers (Two’s Complement)

Two’s complement is the most common method to represent negative numbers in binary.

  1. Invert all bits (one’s complement)
  2. Add 1
Example: Represent −5 in 8-bit binary:
+5 = 00000101
Invert: 11111010
Add 1 → 11111011
