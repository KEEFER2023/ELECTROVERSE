📡 Electromagnetic Waves

This chapter dives into the nature, generation, propagation, and practical usage of electromagnetic (EM) waves — crucial for wireless communication, optics, radar, and power transmission.

34. What Are Electromagnetic Waves?

EM waves are oscillating electric (E) and magnetic (B) fields that travel through space, generated by accelerating charges.

c = 1 / \sqrt{\mu_0 \varepsilon_0} → speed of light in vacuum (~3×10⁸ m/s)

35. Maxwell’s Equations (Simplified View)

  • Gauss for E: ∇·E = ρ/ε₀
  • Gauss for B: ∇·B = 0
  • Faraday’s Law: ∇×E = -∂B/∂t
  • Ampère–Maxwell Law: ∇×B = μ₀J + μ₀ε₀ ∂E/∂t
Combining the last two yields wave equations for E and B!

36. Wave Equation in Free Space

∇²E = μ₀ε₀ ∂²E/∂t² and same for B.

Solutions are sinusoidal plane waves: E(x,t) = E₀ sin(kx - ωt)

37. Properties of EM Waves

  • Transverse: E ⊥ B ⊥ direction of propagation
  • Speed: c = fλ
  • Energy transported via Poynting vector S = E × H
  • No medium needed: can travel in vacuum

38. Electromagnetic Spectrum

  • Radio (kHz–MHz): communications
  • Microwave (GHz): radar, cooking
  • Infrared: heat sensing
  • Visible light: human vision (400–700 nm)
  • Ultraviolet: sterilization, astronomy
  • X-rays, Gamma: medical, nuclear
Low frequency ⟶⟶⟶⟶⟶⟶⟶ High frequency

39. Reflection, Refraction, Diffraction

  • Reflection: Wave bounces (θi = θr)
  • Refraction: Change in speed → bends (Snell’s Law)
  • Diffraction: Spreading around obstacles

40. Polarization

Direction of E-field vector in wave. Can be linear, circular, or elliptical.

Used in antennas, sunglasses, LCD screens

41. Real Engineering Applications

  • Wireless communication (5G, Wi-Fi, radio)
  • Optics and fiber transmission
  • Radar, satellite, remote sensing
  • Medical imaging (X-ray, MRI)
  • EM shielding in circuits

42. Practice Questions

Q1: Find frequency of wave with λ = 600 nm

Ans: f = c/λ = 5×10¹⁴ Hz

Q2: If E₀ = 100 V/m in vacuum, B₀ = ?

Ans: B₀ = E₀ / c ≈ 3.33×10⁻⁷ T

Q3: What's the speed of EM wave in medium with μ = 2μ₀, ε = 3ε₀?

Ans: v = 1 / √(με) = c / √6
