Circuit Analysis

Ohm's Law

Formula: V = I × R

This fundamental law relates voltage (V), current (I), and resistance (R). It forms the foundation of all circuit analysis.

Kirchhoff’s Laws

KCL (Current Law): The sum of currents entering a node equals the sum of currents leaving the node.

KVL (Voltage Law): The sum of voltage drops around any closed loop is zero.

Series & Parallel Circuits

Series: Current is the same through all components, total resistance adds up.

Parallel: Voltage is the same across branches, total resistance is given by 1/Req = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + ...

Thevenin & Norton Theorems

Thevenin: Any linear electrical network can be replaced by an equivalent circuit with a single voltage source and series resistance.

Norton: Any linear electrical network can also be replaced by an equivalent circuit with a current source and parallel resistance.
