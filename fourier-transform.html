Fourier Transforms

Fourier Transforms break down signals into their frequency components — a cornerstone concept in signal processing and communications.

EE Insight: Analyze filters, communication signals, and frequency-domain behavior with the FT.

1. Definitions

Continuous Fourier Transform (FT):

X(f) = ∫₋∞⁺∞ x(t) e−j2πft dt

Inverse FT: x(t) = ∫₋∞⁺∞ X(f) ej2πft df

Tip: f is frequency in Hz. Use angular form (ω = 2πf) when preferred.

2. Common Fourier Transform Pairs

x(t)X(f)Notes
δ(t)1All frequencies present
1δ(f)DC only
e−a tu(t)1/(a + j2πf)a > 0
sin(2πf₀t)(1/2j)[δ(f−f₀) − δ(f+f₀)]Pure tone
cos(2πf₀t)(1/2)[δ(f−f₀) + δ(f+f₀)]Even-toned
Practice these by sketching frequency spikes for sinusoids and exponentials.

3. Properties

  • Linearity: FT{ax + by} = aX + bY
  • Time shift: FT{x(t−t₀)} = X(f)e−j2πft₀
  • Frequency shift: FT{x(t)ej2πf₀t} = X(f−f₀)
  • Convolution: FT{x * h} = X(f)·H(f)
  • Parseval’s theorem: Energy in time equals energy in freq: ∫|x|² = ∫|X|²

5. Exam‑Style Example

x(t) = e−a tu(t)
X(f) = ∫₀⁺∞ e−ate−j2πft dt = 1 / (a + j2πf)
Conclusion: Spectrum is real axis-shifted pole → low-pass behavior.

6. Interactive Quiz

Q1: FT of δ(t−2) = ?

Solution: e−j2πf·2

Q2: FT of cos(2π·50t) = ?

Solution: (1/2)[δ(f−50) + δ(f+50)]

Q3: Given x(t)=e−2tu(t), find X(f)

Solution: ∫₀∞ e−2te−j2πft dt = 1 / (2 + j2πf)
