üì° Signal Types

Signals are functions that convey information about the behavior or attributes of some phenomenon. In electrical engineering, signals are often functions of time and are used to represent electrical quantities like voltage and current.

üìë Classification of Signals

  • Continuous-Time vs Discrete-Time
    • Continuous-Time Signal: Defined at every instant in time (e.g., sin(t), cos(t)).
    • Discrete-Time Signal: Defined only at discrete time intervals (e.g., x[n]).
  • Analog vs Digital
    • Analog Signal: Takes any value in a continuous range.
    • Digital Signal: Takes discrete values (e.g., binary 0 and 1).
  • Deterministic vs Random
    • Deterministic: Can be described with a precise formula (e.g., sin(2œÄt)).
    • Random: Cannot be described by a fixed formula (e.g., noise).
  • Periodic vs Aperiodic
    • Periodic: Repeats after a fixed interval T (e.g., sin(t)).
    • Aperiodic: Does not repeat (e.g., exponential decay).

  • Energy vs Power
    • Energy Signal: Has finite total energy.
    • Power Signal: Has finite power over any interval.
